## PPT 4

4 corruption offences

1. solicit and accept bribe
2. offer or give bribe
3. false claim
4. abuse of power by a public officer

**corruption**

imprisonment $\leq$ 20 years

fine the value of gratification $\times 5$ or RM 10000 whichever is higher

**money laundering**

imprisonment $\leq$ 15 years

fine the value of gratification $\times 5$ or RM 5M whichever is higher

**receive gifts by a public servant**

2 years' imprisonment or fine or both

gifts $\rightarrow$ bribes

- the giver and recipient of gifts has official relations
- the giving of gifts influences the decision that will be made by the recipient
- the value of the gift is not commensurate with the gift

## PPT 7

responsiblity to lodge a report

fail to report offer/giving of bribes

- fine $\leq$ RM 100000
- or imprisonment $\leq$ 10 years
- or both

fail to report solicitation/receiving of bribes

- fine $\leq$ RM 10000
- or imprisonment $\leq$ 2 years
- or both

whistle-blower 内部检举者

witness 证人

8 tips to avoid corruption

1. do not say yes on first offer
2. be careful with compliments
3. do not ever show actions or behaviour that seems to portray that we are asking
4. do not get accustomed to receiving any form of entertainment from those who have official relationship with us, even if it is just a treat to a drink
5. avoid a relationship that is too close with a company that has an official relationship with us
6. decline offer or persuasion firmly
7. avoid talking about personal matters or family issues with people with whom we have an official relations
8. befriend people who are honest and can advice and guide us
